Advanced Topics in Numerical Linear Algebra

This is meant to be a second course in numerical linear algebra, and its purpose is to fill in many of the important gaps typically left in introductory courses, such as accurate symmetric factorizations, partially-reorthogonalized Lanczos, the practical details of dense and condensed-form eigenvalue algorithms, how to efficiently compute pseudospectra, and algorithms for quickly updating matrix factorizations.
