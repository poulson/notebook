Mathematics in the Real World

Introduction to non-calculus applications of mathematical ideas and principles in real-world problems. Topics include probability and the notion of an expectations, estimation with respect to the one and two norms, linearity, basic statistical concepts (e.g., regression), and geometric series. Applications include insurance models, gambling, and high-level techniques for analyzing random and noisy data. No knowledge of calculus required. Enrollment limited to students who do not have Stanford credit for a high school or college course in calculus or statistics.
